When should trees be removed?

Determining when a tree must be removed is critical for property safety, especially in Southeast Idaho. In the high-desert climate of Bannock County, weather extremes—ranging from heavy winter ice loading to intense summer windstorms—put immense structural stress on trees. Common species in Pocatello like the Siberian Elm (known for weak wood and narrow branch angles) and aging Cottonwoods near the Portneuf River are highly prone to sudden limb failure.

Key signs that indicate a tree has become a hazard and requires removal include deep trunk decay, large dead limbs, root damage from utility trenching, or a noticeable lean toward structures. If a tree shows signs of severe stress or disease, such as bark splitting or fungal growth at the base, it's vital to have it evaluated. Removing a compromised tree before the next seasonal storm prevents costly property damage and safety risks in residential areas like Indian Hills, Highland, and the University neighborhood.
