Tree Removal Process

Taking down a tree—especially a large one near power lines or homes—is a highly technical task. At Pocatello Tree Service, safety is our absolute priority. We utilize professional rigging techniques, heavy-duty lowering ropes, block-and-tackle systems, and bucket trucks to ensure controlled, piece-by-piece dismantling of hazardous trees. This prevents any impact on neighboring properties, fences, or landscaping.

Our professional tree removal process includes:

  • Comprehensive Risk Assessment: We evaluate the tree’s species, lean, wood decay, and proximity to structures to map out a precise felling or rigging plan.
  • Advanced Tree Rigging: For tight residential spots, our climbers scale the tree and use arborist rigging blocks and pulleys to lower large branches safely and slowly.
  • Stump Grinding Prep: Once the main trunk is cut as close to the ground as possible, we prep the site for stump grinding to completely clear the root system.
  • Thorough Clean-up: We haul away all logs, branches, and wood chips, leaving your property cleaner than we found it.

When should trees be removed?

Determining when a tree must be removed is critical for property safety, especially in Southeast Idaho. In the high-desert climate of Bannock County, weather extremes—ranging from heavy winter ice loading to intense summer windstorms—put immense structural stress on trees. Common species in Pocatello like the Siberian Elm (known for weak wood and narrow branch angles) and aging Cottonwoods near the Portneuf River are highly prone to sudden limb failure.

Key signs that indicate a tree has become a hazard and requires removal include deep trunk decay, large dead limbs, root damage from utility trenching, or a noticeable lean toward structures. If a tree shows signs of severe stress or disease, such as bark splitting or fungal growth at the base, it's vital to have it evaluated. Removing a compromised tree before the next seasonal storm prevents costly property damage and safety risks in residential areas like Indian Hills, Highland, and the University neighborhood.
